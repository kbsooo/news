# US Defence Tech Shake-Up — Neo-Primes (2026)

## The Lesson That Triggered It

The Iran war taught the US a painful economics lesson: **using $1m missiles to shoot down $50,000 Iranian drones is not viable**. Pentagon official Emil Michael (former Silicon Valley executive): *"You don't want to spend a $1m missile to take out a $50,000 drone."* This single observation has accelerated a procurement reorientation that was already underway.

## The Three "Neo-Primes"

A clique of defence upstarts now positioned as challengers to the legacy primes (Lockheed Martin, RTX, Northrop Grumman):

- **Palantir** — software giant; AI-infused command-and-control system **Maven** designated a "programme of record" in March (locks in funding for years)
- **SpaceX** — **Starshield** satellite network for reconnaissance and connectivity; expected to list shares in **the largest IPO of all time** in coming months
- **Anduril** — drones, anti-drone weapons; in March America's army consolidated multiple Anduril contracts into one **worth up to $20bn over 10 years**; raising at **$60bn valuation** despite **only $2bn revenue and a loss** last year

Behind them, a second tier raising big VC cheques: **Shield AI** (autonomous combat pilots), **Saronic** (maritime drones).

## Scale Asymmetry — Valuation Outpaces Revenue

| | Three legacy primes | Three neo-primes |
|---|---|---|
| Combined annual sales | ~8x neo-primes | Tiny by comparison |
| Combined market valuation | 1x | **>3x legacy primes** |

Investors are pricing in disruption that has barely begun.

## Why the Pentagon Is Buying In

### Contract structure: fixed-price vs cost-plus
- **Legacy primes** rely on **cost-plus** contracts (govt reimburses all expenses + margin) — works for huge programmes (e.g. F-35: **$2trn+ over decades**) but invites sloth
- **Neo-primes** prefer **fixed-price** deals — eat their own R&D, earn fat margins if they deliver on time/budget; this keeps them lean and incentivises iteration

### Speed evidence
- **8 months** from SpektreWorks unveiling **LUCAS** suicide-drone prototype to deployment in Iran (LUCAS is, ironically, a reverse-engineered copy of an Iranian Shahed)
- Anduril plans common rocket motor with solid propellants reused across launch systems

### Trump-administration alignment
- **Pete Hegseth** ([[donald-trump|War Secretary]]) used SpaceX's Texas base to release a new AI strategy in January, promising the Department of War would draw inspiration from Musk's management approach and "accelerate like hell"
- Trump's **$1.5trn defence-budget** push (a **40%+ increase** on current levels) — neo-primes hope their current 1-2% share rises by "percentage points" annually

## Risks — For the Challengers

- **Manufacturing scale-up**: Anduril is the most exposed; just opened a $1bn Ohio factory; ability to ramp to legacy-prime volumes **untested**. SpaceX/Palantir have other commercial scale to draw on
- **Becoming the new legacy primes**: Anduril is already a serial acquirer (bought Blue Force Technologies / Fury combat aircraft in 2023). Scott Bledsoe, Fury inventor: *"There is a danger that all we are doing is creating a new breed of legacy primes."* An insider when asked about state-by-state lobbying: **"Hell yeah."**

## Risks — For the Government

- **Lock-in**: the Pentagon wants interoperable systems but could become locked into SpaceX launch services, or Palantir/Anduril battlefield-management
- **Drone myopia**: too much enthusiasm for cheap drones could **deprioritise long-range, advanced-defence-penetrating systems** essential for a future China conflict in the Pacific (drones don't easily cross oceans)
- **Closed door for newcomers**: despite rhetoric of "more competition," it's still hard for the next tier to enter — Anduril acquires them instead

## Risks — For Society

The cosying-up between the [[donald-trump|Trump]] family and the neo-primes creates governance and political-risk issues:
- When Palantir shares were hit by short-sellers, Trump posted defence on Truth Social including the ticker: **"Palantir Technologies (PLTR) has proven to have great war-fighting capabilities and equipment. Just ask our enemies!!!"**
- **Donald Trump Jr.** is a partner at **1789 Capital**, a VC firm that has invested in Anduril
- Neo-prime executive **Matthew Steckman**: *"Every investor on the planet is an investor in Anduril."* — true in the sense of broad index ownership, but does not address the conflict-of-interest perception
- **Reversibility risk**: Democratic support for defence startups remains broadly bipartisan, but if the neo-primes are perceived as Trump-family vehicles, **Republican-loss-of-power scenarios** put them in danger of being targeted

## Why It Matters

The shake-up is **structurally overdue** — the legacy military-industrial complex is slow, expensive, and risk-averse. If it succeeds, it will:
- Cut weapon-system costs and accelerate iteration
- Shift the [[great-power-rivalry|US-China]] competition toward software-and-drone-centric warfare (where the US starts with an AI advantage)
- Reduce the per-unit economics asymmetry that punishes the West in drone warfare (the lesson of the [[iran-war-and-ceasefire-2026|Iran war]])

If it fails or misfires, it leaves a **partisan-vulnerable**, **lock-in-prone** new oligopoly that may be no less rent-seeking than the one it replaces.

## Arsenal-of-Democracy Editorial (Apr 22, 2026) — Institutional Synthesis

*The Economist* Leaders promoted the neo-prime thesis to institutional editorial position. Core claims:

### Unified lesson from Ukraine + Iran wars
- Ukraine: pioneered low-cost drones with **weekly software updates** against larger Russian army
- Israel/US used **F-35, B-2 bombers, air-launched ballistic missiles, refueling tankers** vs Iran
- **Common thread**: whether big/small, expensive/cheap, crewed/uncrewed, **what matters is the software** — best algorithms, best data, updated most frequently

### Three explicit messages
1. **Western defence-manufacturing capacity insufficient**: America used up **half its stocks of high-end air-defence munitions in 40 days** of war
2. **Balance high-end + cheap-replaceable**: a few exquisite systems, many cheap/replaceable ones
3. **Software primacy**: regardless of form factor, software is the binding constraint

### Palantir = most valuable defence prime
- Palantir now worth **more than RTX** (formerly Raytheon, most valuable legacy prime)
- Together with SpaceX and Anduril, **"neo-prime" trio**
- Behave as software-first; draw from modern manufacturing (including car industry) for the physical products
- Hungrier than incumbents; heavy in-house R&D

### Hegseth's procurement reforms (from Nov 2024)
- **Buy things faster** — speeding up Byzantine processes
- Give **front-line commands greater say** in what gets bought
- Draw **more from commercial technology** vs bespoke
- These create conditions for competition with legacy primes — mercifully, "the right ideas about procurement" despite Hegseth's broader chaos

### Europe's structural problem
- **Hardly any big new defence-tech companies**
- Small innovative firms **stifled** by fragmented market, limited VC, low government demand
- **Germany's €100bn special fund — nearly all on traditional kit**
- Britain's defence review: 10% to "novel" technology — **some insiders argued F-35 (20 years old) should count**
- **Skycutter** (British drone co) won Pentagon contract; may leave Britain for lack of UK orders

### What Europe should do — "lean in" to Ukraine
- Britain-Ukraine: interceptor drone co-development
- Germany-Ukraine: new drone agreement
- **Rheinmetall + Destinus** (Netherlands) JV: long-range missiles at fraction of cost of other models
- Ukraine offered to **share battlefield data for AI training** — valuable commodity
- Editorial: *"Much more of this is necessary if the arsenal of democracy is not to depend on a handful of American firms"*

### Strategic framing
The editorial reframes neo-primes not as a trading opportunity but as a **democracy-strategic** necessity. Two clauses are tightly linked:
- (a) autocracies are rearming (China's annual increase = Taiwan's entire budget); this is NOT optional
- (b) **arms not budgets deter** — Europe's €300bn/year extra doesn't translate unless procurement + industrial base shift

## Cross-Connections

- The drone-economics insight echoes [[ukraine|Ukraine]]'s drone-defence lessons being **exported to the Gulf** ($2-5K interceptors vs $50K Shaheds) — same logic, different supplier
- Compare with [[european-defence-industry]] — Europe is also rearming but through a different model (CSG €6.7bn, government-guaranteed orders, less VC-driven)
- Connects to [[ai-labs-and-industry]] — Palantir/Anduril are AI-product applications outside the frontier-model labs; they are arguably the **biggest US AI deployment vector** for the next 5 years
- Connects to [[ai-regulation-mythos-moment]] — AI safety regulation that constrains commercial models will likely **carve out** military applications, potentially expanding neo-prime advantage

# 미 방산 테크 격변 — 네오프라임 (2026)

## 촉발한 교훈

이란 전쟁이 미국에 가르친 뼈아픈 경제학 교훈: **5만 달러짜리 이란 드론을 격추하기 위해 100만 달러짜리 미사일을 쓰는 것은 지속 불가능**. 펜타곤 관계자 에밀 마이클(전 실리콘밸리 임원): *"5만 달러 드론을 잡으려고 100만 달러짜리 미사일을 쏘고 싶진 않다."* 이 한 문장이 이미 진행 중이던 조달 재편을 가속화했다.

## 세 곳의 "네오프라임"

기존 프라임(록히드마틴, RTX, 노스럽그러먼)에 도전하는 신예 방산기업 클리크:

- **Palantir** — 소프트웨어 거인; AI 기반 지휘통제 시스템 **Maven**이 3월 "프로그램 오브 레코드"로 지정 (수년간 자금 락인)
- **SpaceX** — 정찰·통신용 **Starshield** 위성망; 향후 **사상 최대 IPO** 상장 예정
- **Anduril** — 드론·대드론 무기; 3월 미 육군이 다수 Anduril 계약을 **10년간 최대 200억 달러** 단일 계약으로 통합; **매출 20억 달러·적자**임에도 **600억 달러 가치 평가**로 자금 조달 중

후위 2진(VC 거액 유치): **Shield AI**(자율 공중전투 조종), **Saronic**(해상 드론).

## 가치-매출 비대칭

| | 레거시 프라임 3사 | 네오프라임 3사 |
|---|---|---|
| 합산 매출 | 네오프라임의 ~8배 | 비교 시 미미 |
| 합산 시가총액 | 1배 | **레거시의 3배 초과** |

투자자들은 아직 거의 시작되지도 않은 파괴적 변화를 가격에 반영 중.

## 펜타곤이 동의한 이유

### 계약 구조: 고정가 vs 원가가산
- **레거시**는 **원가가산(cost-plus)** 계약(정부가 비용 전액 + 마진 보전) — 거대 프로그램(예: F-35 **수십 년에 걸쳐 2조 달러+**)에 적합하나 게으름 유발
- **네오프라임**은 **고정가** 계약 선호 — 자체 R&D 부담; 일정·예산 내 인도 시 두꺼운 마진. 린함과 빠른 반복을 강제

### 속도 증거
- **SpektreWorks**가 자살 드론 **LUCAS** 프로토타입을 공개한 후 **8개월 만에** 이란 전장에 배치 (LUCAS는 아이러니컬하게 이란 Shahed 역설계 카피)
- Anduril은 다양한 발사 시스템에 공용 고체연료 모터 사용 계획

### 트럼프 행정부와의 정렬
- **피트 헤그세스** 전쟁부 장관이 1월 SpaceX 텍사스 기지를 배경으로 새 AI 전략 발표, 머스크의 경영 방식을 따라 "지옥처럼 가속하겠다"고 약속
- 트럼프의 **1.5조 달러 국방예산** 추진(현재 대비 **40%+ 증액**) — 네오프라임은 현재 1-2% 점유율이 매년 "수%포인트" 증가하길 기대

## 도전자들의 리스크

- **양산 스케일업**: Anduril이 가장 노출됨; 오하이오 10억 달러 공장 막 가동; 레거시 프라임 수준 양산 능력 **검증 안 됨**. SpaceX/Palantir는 다른 상업 매출 기반 보유
- **새로운 레거시 프라임이 됨**: Anduril은 이미 연쇄 인수(2023년 Blue Force Tech / Fury 전투기 인수). Scott Bledsoe, Fury 발명자: *"우리가 지금 하는 일이 새로운 종류의 레거시 프라임을 만드는 것일 위험이 있다."* 한 네오프라임 내부자가 주별 로비 활동 가능성에 대해 답변: **"Hell yeah."**

## 정부의 리스크

- **락인**: 펜타곤은 상호운용 시스템을 원하지만 SpaceX 발사 서비스나 Palantir/Anduril 전장관리 시스템에 종속될 수 있음
- **드론 근시안**: 값싼 드론에 대한 과도한 열광이 미래 중국 태평양 분쟁에 필수적인 **장거리·고급 방어망 침투 시스템**을 우선순위에서 밀어낼 위험
- **신규 진입 장벽**: "더 많은 경쟁" 수사에도 불구하고, 차세대 진입은 여전히 어려움 — Anduril이 그들을 대신 인수

## 사회적 리스크

[[donald-trump|트럼프]] 가족과 네오프라임의 친밀화는 거버넌스·정치 리스크 야기:
- Palantir 공매도 공격 시 트럼프가 트루스소셜에 티커 포함 옹호글: **"Palantir Technologies (PLTR)는 위대한 전쟁 수행 역량과 장비를 입증했다. 우리 적들에게 물어보라!!!"**
- **도널드 트럼프 주니어**가 Anduril에 투자한 VC 펌 **1789 Capital**의 파트너
- 네오프라임 임원 **Matthew Steckman**: *"지구상 모든 투자자가 Anduril의 투자자다."* — 광범위한 인덱스 보유라는 의미로는 사실이나, 이해 충돌 인식을 해소하지는 못함
- **가역성 리스크**: 민주당의 방산 스타트업 지원은 폭넓게 초당적이지만, 네오프라임이 트럼프 가족 비히클로 인식된다면 **공화당 권력 상실 시나리오**에서 표적이 될 위험

## 왜 중요한가

이번 격변은 **구조적으로 시급함** — 기존 군산복합체는 느리고 비싸고 위험회피적. 성공 시:
- 무기 시스템 비용 절감 및 반복 가속
- [[great-power-rivalry|미-중]] 경쟁을 소프트웨어·드론 중심 전쟁으로 전환(미국이 AI 우위에서 시작)
- 드론전에서 서방을 처벌해온 단가 비대칭 해소(이란 전쟁의 교훈)

실패 시 **당파 취약**·**락인 경향**의 새로운 과점 체제가 남으며, 대체된 것보다 덜 지대 추구적이리라는 보장이 없다.

## 교차 연결

- 드론 경제학 통찰은 [[ukraine|우크라이나]]의 드론 방어 경험이 걸프로 **수출**되는 흐름과 공명($2-5K 요격기 vs $50K Shahed)
- [[european-defence-industry]]와 비교 — 유럽도 재무장 중이나 다른 모델(CSG 67억 유로, 정부 보장 주문, VC 의존도 낮음)
- [[ai-labs-and-industry]]와 연결 — Palantir/Anduril은 프론티어 모델 랩 외부의 AI 제품 응용; 향후 5년 **미 AI 배치 최대 벡터**일 수 있음
- [[ai-regulation-mythos-moment]]와 연결 — 상업 모델을 제약하는 AI 안전 규제는 군사 응용에 대해 **예외**를 둘 가능성이 높아, 네오프라임의 우위를 확장할 수 있음
