## Analysis
## The Emergent Muscat Framework: How Collapse Bent Toward Stabilization

The central surprise of this 5-round simulation is that the **April 12 collapse of the Islamabad talks did not lead to renewed war** — it led, paradoxically, to a *deeper and more durable* regional settlement than the original ceasefire produced. When seven of eight agents independently arrived at de-escalation paths in Round 1-2, the resulting architecture (the 'Muscat Framework') stabilized progressively through Rounds 3-5, eventually institutionalizing a post-war Middle East order. Iran shipped 250kg of its 400kg HEU to Russia for LEU conversion; enrichment capped at 3.67%; $6bn frozen Qatari funds released; a $50bn Gulf reconstruction fund for Iran; and in August, Saudi-Israel 'Abraham 2.0' normalization signed — the single biggest diplomatic reshuffle since Camp David. The strait stabilized at ~95 transits/day (76% of pre-war), and Brent fell from $108 at peak tension to $72 by August 2026. This outcome was not preordained: the simulation suggests a ~55% probability pathway, with catastrophic alternatives (US-China naval clash over Chinese-flagged tankers, Israeli unilateral strike on Fordow, Iranian hardliner coup against Qalibaf) each carrying 10-20% tail probability.

## The Three Forcing Functions That Bent the Curve

Three mechanisms did the heavy lifting. **First**, China's PLAN escort announcement in Round 1 (protecting Chinese-flagged Iranian crude tankers) converted Trump's unilateral blockade order into a direct US-China confrontation risk four weeks before the May 14 Xi summit — an unacceptable cost that forced Trump into de-escalation theater he could spin as victory. **Second**, the Gulf states' full $6bn Qatar release plus the $50bn reconstruction fund converted Iran's pragmatist faction (Qalibaf) into the dominant voice against hardliners: cash and sanctions relief flowing NOW is worth more than toll revenue or enrichment prestige. **Third**, Pakistan's shuttle diplomacy plus Korea's middle-power 'coalition of the damaged' created diplomatic scaffolding that let Trump take the off-ramp without owning the climbdown. The turning point was Round 2 (Apr 16-22): once the Muscat Framework was signed and the ceasefire extended in writing to May 22, path dependency kicked in — every subsequent round made unwinding more costly for every participant.

## Who Won, Who Lost, What Changed Structurally

**Clear winners**: China (+20 influence points; co-guarantor status, RMB-oil pilot, BRI Gulf expansion, cheap Iranian crude filling SPR); Gulf states (+45 stability; Vision 2030 revived, GCC-Iran nonaggression pact, $80bn infrastructure, pipeline alternatives built); Pakistan (mediator brand permanent, IMF bridge, F-16 upgrade, Trump Nobel co-nomination); South Korea (+3 effective — energy crisis averted, KOREUS chip trilateral, 4 new nuclear reactors approved, KOSPI 5,778→6,500). **Qualified winners**: Trump (claims 'historic Iran deal' + Abraham 2.0 for midterms, but Nobel + Warsh Fed rolled into short-term wins; US credibility only partially recovered 35→48); Iran (Qalibaf consolidated, funds flowing, Gulf normalized — but lost toll leverage and most HEU). **Clear losers**: NATO/Europe (-2 cohesion; fully excluded from post-war deal-making, watching Trump-Xi-Arab order form without them); Netanyahu's hardliner coalition (forced onto Abraham 2.0 track, hidden-HEU card held as 2027 leverage only); Iranian hardliners (Qalibaf's pragmatist faction ascendant via IRGC reshuffle). **Structural changes**: Hormuz is now a dual-track insurance regime (Western P&I + Sinosure+PLAN); Fujairah STS hub becomes permanent global infrastructure; petrodollar gets its first structural crack via RMB oil pilot; Muscat Framework Secretariat institutionalizes Oman + Pakistan as regional convenors.

## The Hidden Risks That Did Not Trigger

Four tail risks were live through all five rounds and did not realize — each worth flagging for post-hoc vigilance. **(1) Iran's hidden 50-80kg HEU cache**, moved to deeper concealment in R4, remained undetected but remains a 2027 rupture candidate if leaked or if Iranian politics shifts. **(2) Russia's slow-walk on LEU conversion** succeeded in buying Iran time past the Israeli 30-day clock but could become a deal-breaker if conversion stalls entirely. **(3) Israeli unilateral strike on Fordow** — Netanyahu's Apr 27 ultimatum window was redirected into the US security package + Abraham 2.0 carrot, but Mossad's Stuxnet-3 operation continues covertly, and a failed covert operation getting exposed could re-trigger crisis. **(4) Iranian hardliner counter-coup against Qalibaf** — the simulation assumes Qalibaf survives two internal challenges; if he falls, the entire pragmatist architecture unwinds. The 2027 Iranian presidential election is the single biggest residual risk.

But it depends on several individually-plausible-but-jointly-fragile conditions: no US-China sea incident during the PLAN-escort period, Qalibaf surviving hardliner pressure, Russia following through on LEU conversion, Netanyahu prioritizing Abraham 2.0 over HEU elimination, and no exogenous shock (Israeli leader shift, Iranian coup, Chinese economic accident). ## Emergent Muscat Framework: 결렬이 어떻게 안정화로 휘어졌는가

이번 5라운드 시뮬레이션의 중심 반전은 **4월 12일 이슬라마바드 회담 결렬이 전쟁 재개로 이어지지 않았다**는 점이다. 역설적으로 원래의 휴전보다 *더 깊고 더 지속가능한* 지역 합의로 이어졌다. 1-2라운드에서 8명의 행위자 중 7명이 독립적으로 데에스컬레이션 경로로 수렴하자, 그 결과 구성된 구조('무스카트 프레임워크')는 3-5라운드를 거치며 점진적으로 안정화되어 결국 전후 중동 질서로 제도화되었다. 이란은 400kg HEU 중 250kg을 러시아로 보내 LEU 전환; 농축 3.67% 상한; 카타르 동결자산 60억 달러 해제; 이란 재건용 500억 달러 걸프 기금; 그리고 8월에 사우디-이스라엘 '아브라함 2.0' 정상화 체결 — 캠프데이비드 이후 최대의 외교 재편이다. 호르무즈는 하루 ~95척 통항으로 안정화(전쟁 전 대비 76%)되었고, 브렌트유는 피크 $108에서 8월 $72로 하락했다. 이 결과가 필연적이었던 것은 아니다. 시뮬레이션은 이 경로를 약 55% 확률로 추정하며, 파국 시나리오(중국 깃발 탱커를 둘러싼 미-중 해상 충돌, 포르도에 대한 이스라엘 단독 공습, 이란 강경파의 Qalibaf 축출 쿠데타)는 각각 10-20%의 꼬리확률을 차지한다.

## 곡선을 휘게 한 3가지 강제 메커니즘

세 가지 메커니즘이 주된 역할을 했다. **첫째**, 1라운드 중국의 PLAN 호위 선언(중국 깃발 이란 원유 탱커 보호)은 트럼프의 일방적 봉쇄 명령을 5월 14일 시진핑 정상회담 4주 전 미-중 직접 충돌 위험으로 전환시켰다 — 이는 받아들일 수 없는 비용이었고, 트럼프는 '승리'로 포장 가능한 데에스컬레이션 연극에 몰렸다. **둘째**, 걸프국들의 카타르 60억 달러 전액 해제 + 500억 달러 재건기금은 이란 실용파(Qalibaf)를 강경파에 맞서는 지배적 목소리로 전환시켰다: *지금 들어오는 현금과 제재 해제*가 통행료 수입이나 농축 위세보다 가치 있었다. **셋째**, 파키스탄의 셔틀 외교 + 한국의 중견국 '피해자 연합'은 트럼프가 굴복을 뒤집어쓰지 않고 출구를 잡을 수 있는 외교적 비계(scaffolding)를 만들었다. 전환점은 2라운드(4월 16-22)였다: 무스카트 프레임워크가 서명되고 휴전이 문서로 5월 22일까지 연장된 순간, 경로 의존성이 작동했다 — 이후 매 라운드마다 되돌리는 비용이 모든 참여자에게 더 커졌다.
