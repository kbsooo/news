## Round-by-Round Summary

### Round 1: May 2026

**Actions:**
- **Iran**: negotiate → Sends delegation to Islamabad talks but with maximalist demands: asset unfreezing ($120bn+), recognition of maritime control over Hormuz, Israel ceasefire in Lebanon. Pragmatists lead, but hardliners ensure no real concessions. Simultaneously formalizes toll collection — IRGC naval units now staffed for 24/7 processing. Transit rises slightly to 15 ships/day as paying vessels are cleared.
- **Donald Trump**: negotiate → Attends Xi-Trump summit in Beijing (May 2026). Trades partial tariff relief for Chinese pressure on Iran. Privately floats 'joint venture' toll-sharing idea again, infuriating Gulf allies. Publicly demands 'FULL OPENING' but takes no military action. Inflation at 3.3% becoming domestic political liability.
- **China**: cooperate → Hosts Xi-Trump summit. Extracts tariff concessions and framework for Chinese investment in US. Continues buying Iranian oil via shadow fleet at discounted prices. Quietly expands green tech export deals with Gulf states and South Asia. Strategic oil reserves (1.3bn barrels) provide cushion — no urgency to resolve crisis.
- **Gulf States**: invest → Saudi Arabia completes emergency Yanbu pipeline repair — restored to 5m bbl/day (from 7m pre-strike). UAE maximizes Fujairah pipeline to 1.5m bbl/day. Signs emergency defense agreements with South Korea (air defense) and Turkey (naval patrol). Begins bilateral secret talks with Iranian pragmatists. Total Gulf exports recover to ~3m bbl/day via pipelines (vs 18m pre-war through strait).
- **South Korea**: cooperate → Emergency LNG contracts signed with Australia and US Gulf Coast — first cargoes arrive late May. Emergency budget ($15bn supplemental) passes. Gas rationing for industry begins. Samsung/SK Hynix draw down helium stockpiles; Lee government negotiates emergency bromine shipments from Israel via air freight. Defense deal with UAE: deploy Cheongung air defense systems in exchange for priority energy access.
- **Israel**: escalate → Continues Lebanon strikes despite ceasefire framework — 100+ additional strikes killing 200+ in May. Netanyahu hosts Trump for Independence Day visit, secures backing for 'finishing the job' against Hezbollah. Iran cites Israeli Lebanon strikes as justification for keeping strait restricted. Israeli action directly undermines broader peace process.

**Impact:** May 2026: Islamabad talks begin but Iran's maximalist position ensures no breakthrough. The Xi-Trump summit produces limited tariff relief but no coordinated Iran pressure — China benefits most. Gulf states pivot to pipeline alternatives and new security partners, recovering ~3m bbl/day vs 18m pre-war. South Korea enters emergency mode — energy rationing plus emergency procurement. Israel's continued Lebanon escalation gives Iran cover to maintain strait restrictions, creating a vicious cycle. Oil rises to ~$102 as markets realize the blockade isn't ending soon. Food security deteriorates as fertilizer shipments remain mostly blocked.

- **South Korea**: invest → Emergency LNG cargoes arrive from Australia and US, easing acute gas shortage. Government fast-tracks 3 nuclear reactor approvals. Samsung delays 2 non-critical memory chip lines due to helium rationing. SK Hynix develops helium recycling process (recovering 40% vs typical 15%). Lee announces 'Energy Independence 2035' plan redirecting $100bn from chip subsidies toward nuclear + renewables.
- **Israel**: de-escalate → Under intense US pressure (and with elections approaching), scales back Lebanon strikes to 'targeted operations' — 20-30 strikes/month vs 100+ in May. Israel-Lebanon talks in Washington produce framework for reduced Hezbollah presence south of Litani River. But IDF maintains freedom to strike 'imminent threats.' Partial de-escalation removes one of Iran's justifications for strait closure.

**Impact:** June 2026: Iran codifies its Hormuz control into law — a major escalation in form even as transit marginally increases. Trump's secondary sanctions create chaos but are unenforced against major economies. China seizes the diplomatic vacuum with the 'Beijing Framework,' becoming the default mediator. Gulf states pursue parallel tracks: secret Iran deal + massive pipeline investment. South Korea stabilizes energy supply through emergency imports but semiconductor industry starts feeling material shortages. Israel's partial Lebanon de-escalation is the one bright spot — removing a key Iranian excuse. But food security deteriorates sharply as planting season proceeds with 70% less Gulf fertilizer. Oil at $107. The crisis is calcifying into a new normal.

Qatar breaks ground on new pipeline to Oman coast (bypassing strait — 2-year construction timeline).
- **South Korea**: invest → First emergency Australian LNG long-term contract signed (10-year, 3m tonnes/year). Gas rationing ends for most industries. Quietly supports Trump's Iran back-channel as way to claim credit for ending the crisis. Israeli economy recovering — Tel Aviv stock exchange up 12% from war lows.

**Impact:** July 2026: Turning point month. Iran's pragmatists gain ascendancy as toll revenue demonstrates the value of cooperation over confrontation. The Saudi-Iranian 'Riyadh Accord' framework is the biggest diplomatic breakthrough — Gulf states effectively accept limited Iranian maritime role in exchange for economic normalization. Trump pivots to deal-making under domestic political pressure. Israel secures Lebanon deal, removing a key obstacle. China continues accumulating influence through green tech and reconstruction deals. Transit rises to 25 ships/day. Oil dips slightly to $104 on diplomatic optimism. But the IRGC tanker seizure and nuclear acceleration reveal the fragility — Iran's internal factions remain unstable. Food security continues to worsen as reduced-fertilizer crops mature poorly.

## Analysis
## The Blockade That Became a Tollbooth: How Hormuz Reshaped Global Order

The most significant finding of this simulation is that a prolonged Hormuz blockade does not persist as a binary state (open/closed) but evolves into a managed restriction — an Iranian tollbooth on global energy. By December 2026, transit recovers to ~60 ships/day (half of pre-war 125) with reduced tolls ($50k-$150k per vessel), generating $1.8 billion annually for Iran. This outcome reflects the fundamental insight that Iran's pragmatists recognize more revenue flows from a functioning toll system than a closed strait, while hardliners accept it as long as Iran retains gatekeeping leverage. The international community, unable to break Iran's geographic chokepoint through 40 days of bombing, ultimately accommodates this new reality through the Riyadh Accord and Trump-Iran Framework Agreement.

## The Three Structural Shifts

Three irreversible changes define the post-blockade world. First, **China's Middle East dominance**: Beijing's 'builder not bomber' strategy — $60bn+ in reconstruction contracts, the Green Silk Road fund, the Beijing Framework for multilateral governance — has replaced American security hegemony with Chinese economic influence (influence score: 60 → 88). This is the 2020s equivalent of America replacing Britain in the Middle East after Suez 1956. Second, **Gulf energy infrastructure diversification**: pipeline capacity to bypass Hormuz reaches 7m bbl/day by year-end, permanently reducing the strait's chokepoint leverage. The $50bn Arabian Pipeline Initiative ensures that even if Iran re-closes the strait, 40% of Gulf exports can continue. Third, **nuclear proliferation creep**: Iran retains 220kg of HEU (enough for ~5 weapons) as 'insurance,' while Saudi Arabia pursues a Pakistani nuclear umbrella. The war that was supposed to end Iran's nuclear threat has instead created a slow-motion proliferation cascade.

## The Hidden Catastrophe: Food Security

The simulation's gravest outcome is not geopolitical but humanitarian. The 8-month disruption of Gulf fertilizer exports (one-third of global seaborne supply) during the critical planting season produced a global food crisis that will echo through 2027. Wheat yields fell 12%, rice 8%. The World Food Programme's acute hunger estimate rose to 355 million people. Bangladesh lost 4 of 5 domestic fertilizer factories. The connection between energy chokepoints and food security — mediated through natural gas prices → fertilizer costs → crop yields — proved to be the most consequential causal chain of the crisis, more damaging than the oil price shock itself. Food security declined from 40 to 27 (0-100 scale) and was the slowest variable to recover.

## Critical Turning Points and Confidence Assessment

Three pivots shaped the trajectory: (1) **July 2026 — the Riyadh Accord**, where Gulf states pragmatically accepted limited Iranian maritime control in exchange for economic normalization, breaking the diplomatic deadlock; (2) **September 2026 — Iran's Grand Bargain at UNGA**, where domestic political pressure (Trump's midterms, Iran's reconstruction needs) created rare alignment for a deal; (3) **October 2026 — Israel's constraining coalition**, which removed the most dangerous spoiler by tying Netanyahu's hands on unilateral Iran strikes. The simulation assumes rationality prevails over escalation at each of these junctures — a significant assumption given IRGC splinter factions and Trump's unpredictability. Had any of these pivots broken differently (e.g., an IRGC faction sinking a tanker, Trump resuming strikes before midterms, Netanyahu winning a hawkish majority), the trajectory could have been far worse: oil at $130+, food crisis doubling, and potential resumed conflict.

## Year-End Balance Sheet

The 2026 Hormuz crisis produced: oil averaging ~$98/barrel (vs $75 pre-war), global GDP loss of 0.5-1.0%, $25bn in Gulf infrastructure damage, 35-40 million additional people in acute hunger, and a fundamental reordering of Middle Eastern power dynamics. Iran emerges as a tolerated gatekeeper rather than a pariah. America's post-war deal-making partially restored credibility (35 → 44) but the era of unquestioned US security dominance in the Gulf is over. The greatest irony: a war launched to tame Iran and stabilize the Middle East produced the opposite on both counts — Iran gained leverage it never had before, and the region's security architecture was shattered and rebuilt around new poles. The parallels to the 1970s oil shocks are apt: just as those crises catalyzed the fracking revolution and French nuclear power, the 2026 Hormuz crisis will accelerate renewable energy adoption, pipeline diversification, and strategic stockpile expansion for a generation.

## 분석: 봉쇄에서 톨게이트로 — 호르무즈가 세계 질서를 재편하다

### 핵심 발견

이 시뮬레이션의 가장 중요한 발견은 **장기 봉쇄가 이분법적 상태(개방/폐쇄)로 지속되지 않고, '관리된 제한' — 즉 이란의 글로벌 에너지 톨게이트로 진화한다**는 것이다. 2026년 12월까지 통행량은 하루 ~60척(전쟁 전 125척의 절반)으로 회복되며, 축소된 통행료($5만~$15만/척)는 이란에 연간 $18억의 수입을 안겨준다. 이란의 실용주의파는 통행을 늘릴수록 수입이 증가한다는 것을 인식하고, 강경파는 해협 통제권을 유지하는 한 이를 수용한다. 국제사회는 40일간의 폭격으로도 이란의 지리적 병목을 무력화할 수 없음을 깨닫고, 리야드 협정과 트럼프-이란 기본합의를 통해 이 새로운 현실을 수용한다.

### 세 가지 구조적 전환

**1. 중국의 중동 패권 장악** (영향력: 60 → 88): 베이징의 '파괴자가 아닌 건설자' 전략 — $600억+ 재건 계약, 녹색 실크로드 기금, 호르무즈 다자 거버넌스를 위한 베이징 프레임워크 — 이 미국의 안보 헤게모니를 중국의 경제적 영향력으로 대체했다. 이것은 1956년 수에즈 위기 이후 영국을 대체한 미국과 같은 패턴이다.

**2. 걸프 에너지 인프라 다변화**: 호르무즈를 우회하는 파이프라인 용량이 연말까지 700만 bbl/day에 달해, 해협의 병목 레버리지를 영구적으로 감소시킨다. $500억 규모의 아라비안 파이프라인 이니셔티브는 이란이 해협을 재폐쇄하더라도 걸프 수출의 40%가 계속될 수 있도록 보장한다.

**3. 핵 확산의 점진적 진행**: [[iran|이란]]은 220kg의 고농축 우라늄(약 5개 핵무기 분량)을 '보험'으로 보유하고, [[gulf-states|사우디아라비아]]는 파키스탄 핵우산을 추진한다. 이란의 핵 위협을 종식시키기 위한 전쟁이 오히려 느린 확산 연쇄반응을 촉발시켰다.

### 숨겨진 재앙: 식량 안보

시뮬레이션의 가장 심각한 결과는 지정학적이 아닌 인도주의적이다. 걸프 비료 수출(글로벌 해상 공급의 1/3)이 핵심 파종기에 8개월간 중단되면서 2027년까지 반향을 일으킬 글로벌 식량 위기를 초래했다. 밀 수확량 12% 감소, 쌀 8% 감소. 세계식량계획의 급성 기아 추정치는 3억 5,500만 명으로 증가했다. **에너지 병목 → 천연가스 가격 → 비료 비용 → 작물 수확량**으로 이어지는 인과 사슬이 유가 충격 자체보다 더 파괴적이었다.

### 핵심 전환점

세 가지 전환점이 궤적을 결정했다:

1. **2026년 7월 — 리야드 협정**: [[gulf-states|걸프 국가들]]이 경제 정상화를 대가로 이란의 제한적 해양 통제를 실용적으로 수용하며 외교적 교착을 해소
2. **2026년 9월 — 이란의 그랜드 바겐 (UNGA)**: 국내 정치 압력(트럼프의 중간선거, 이란의 재건 필요)이 합의를 위한 드문 정렬을 형성
3. **2026년 10월 — 이스라엘의 제약적 연립정부**: 가장 위험한 스포일러인 네타냐후의 손을 묶어 이란에 대한 일방적 공격을 방지

가장 큰 아이러니: 이란을 길들이고 중동을 안정시키기 위해 시작된 전쟁이 그 정반대의 결과를 낳았다 — 이란은 이전에 없던 레버리지를 얻었고, 지역 안보 구조는 새로운 극들을 중심으로 재편되었다. 1970년대 석유 위기가 셰일혁명과 프랑스 원자력을 촉발시킨 것처럼, 2026년 호르무즈 위기는 한 세대에 걸쳐 재생에너지 채택, 파이프라인 다변화, 전략적 비축 확대를 가속화할 것이다.

### 한국에 대한 시사점

[[south-korea|한국]]은 이 위기에서 **'위기 복원력 있는 중견 강국'** 모델로 부상했다. 가스 고갈 직전에서 호주/미국 LNG 긴급 계약, 원자력 발전소 인허가 신속 처리, 헬륨 재활용 기술 개발, 삼성/SK하이닉스의 공급망 적응을 통해 위기를 관리했다. 2026년 GDP는 -0.5%로 경미한 침체를 겪었지만, AI 수요에 힘입어 반도체 수출은 +22% 성장했다. 이 위기는 한국의 에너지 전략을 **걸프 의존에서 원자력 + 호주/미국 LNG + 재생에너지로 영구적으로 전환**시키는 계기가 되었다.

### 신뢰도: 중간

이 시뮬레이션은 각 전환점에서 합리성이 에스컬레이션을 이긴다고 가정한다. IRGC 분열 세력이 유조선을 격침시키거나, 트럼프가 중간선거 전에 공습을 재개하거나, 네타냐후가 강경 다수를 확보하는 등의 시나리오에서는 궤적이 훨씬 악화될 수 있다: 유가 $130+, 식량 위기 2배, 전쟁 재발 가능성.
